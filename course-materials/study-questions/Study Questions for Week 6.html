Study Questions

These questions are meant to direct your studying for tests and the final exam. When you consider an answer, don't think about only one answer for each question, but rather consider the many different things you've learned that could help answer the question. These questions should get you thinking broadly about the material and how the smaller details fit together and help us build an analysis of how the ancient Greeks and Romans thought about crime and punishment.

  1. How is the field of criminology defined? How do criminologists define their field of study?
  2. What are the various approaches to defining criminology?
  3. How did the field of criminology develop over time?
  4. What is meant by "positivism" and positivist approaches to crime?
  5. How do the three types of positivist approaches differ? What similarities do they share?
  6. How does Walklate explain criminal behaviour? What are the four main approaches she discusses?
  7. Can you explain the criminological and victimological "other"? How does that fit with modern society and ancient society?
