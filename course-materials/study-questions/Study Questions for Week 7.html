Study Questions

These questions are meant to direct your studying for tests and the final exam. When you consider an answer, don't think about only one answer for each question, but rather consider the many different things you've learned that could help answer the question. These questions should get you thinking broadly about the material and how the smaller details fit together and help us build an analysis of how the ancient Greeks and Romans thought about crime and punishment.

  1. What are 'The Five Things' for this week's text?
  2. Who is on trial? Who is the murder victim?
  3. Who is the speaker of the speech for the defence? Who wrote the speech?
  4. What are the relevant provisions of Draco's Homicide Law?
  5. Can you summarize the arguments made by the defence team?
  6. How do those arguments fit with what you know about the procedure and penalties for adultery?
  7. Why does the defence think that their arguments might be successful?
  8. Can you relate aspects of this speech back to what you learned in Walklate and the lecture for Week 6?
  9. Is the approach taken by Lysias similar to or different from what happens in court cases today?
